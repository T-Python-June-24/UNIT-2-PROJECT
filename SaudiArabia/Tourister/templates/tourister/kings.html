History of Saudi Arabian Kings

The lineage of the Saudi family goes back to the Banu Hanifa Al-Bakriya Al-Wailiyya, and the Banu Hanifa are the sons of: Hanifa bin Lujaim bin Saab bin Ali bin Bakr bin Wael bin Qasit bin Hanab bin Afsa bin Du’mi bin Jadila bin Asad bin Rabi’ah bin Nizar bin Ma’ad bin Adnan. As for the spatial aspect, Wadi Al-Ard, known as Wadi Hanifa in reference to Hanifa bin Lujaym, is the headquarters of the tribe of Bani Hanifa bin Lujaym bin Saab bin Ali bin Bakr bin Wael, known for it and known since the pre-Islamic era. Until Wadi Al-Yamamah was called: Wadi Hanifa. From this it becomes clear that the rule of the House of Saud extends its roots back to a long time in the heart of the Arabian Peninsula, and is linked to a long history and a past represented by the nobility of the ruling family and the antiquity of its history.

The timeline highlights the reigns of the Saudi Arabian kings from the founding of modern Saudi Arabia to the present day.

Abdulaziz bin Abdul Rahman Al Saud

Reign: 1932 - 1953



(19 Dhul-Hijjah 1292 AH / 15 January 1876 AD - 2 Rabi’ al-Awwal 1373 AH / 9 November 1953 AD) is the founder of the modern Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, its first king, and the fourteenth ruler of the Al Saud dynasty. He was born in Riyadh to the ruling Al Saud family in Najd.

King Saud bin Abdulaziz Al Saud

Reign: 1953 - 1964


Saud bin Abdulaziz Al Saud (romanized: Suʿūd bin ʿAbd al ʿAzīz Āl Suʿūd; 15 January 1902 – 23 February 1969) was King of Saudi Arabia from 9 November 1953 until his abdication on 2 November 1964. During his reign, he served as Prime Minister of Saudi Arabia from 1953 to 1954 and from 1960 to 1962. Prior to his accession, Saud was the country's crown prince from 11 May 1933 to 9 November 1953. He was the second son of King Abdulaziz, the founder of Saudi Arabia.

King Faisal bin Abdulaziz Al Saud

Reign: 1964 - 1975


Faisal bin Abdulaziz Al Saud (Arabic: فيصل بن عبدالعزيز آل سعود Faṣel bin ʿAbd al ʿAzīz Āl Suʿūd; 14 April 1906 – 25 March 1975) was King of Saudi Arabia from 1964 to 1975. He was the third son of King Abdulaziz. As king, he is credited with rescuing the country's finances and implementing a policy of modernization and reform while his main foreign policy themes were pan-Islamism, anti-Communism, and pro-Palestinian nationalism.

King Khalid bin Abdulaziz Al Saud

Reign: 1975 - 1982


Khalid bin Abdulaziz Al Saud (Arabic: خالد بن عبدالعزيز آل سعود Khālid bin ʿAbd al ʿAzīz Āl Suʿūd; 13 February 1913 – 13 June 1982) was a Saudi Arabian statesman and politician who served as king of Saudi Arabia from 25 March 1975 to 13 June 1982. He was the fourth son of King Abdulaziz, the founder of modern Saudi Arabia.

King Fahd bin Abdulaziz Al Saud

Reign: 1982 - 2005


Fahd bin Abdulaziz Al Saud (Arabic: فهد بن عبد العزيز آل سعود Fahd ibn ‘Abd al-‘Azīz Āl Sa‘ūd, 1921 – 1 August 2005) was King of Saudi Arabia from 13 June 1982 to 1 August 2005. He was the eighth son of King Abdulaziz, the founder of modern Saudi Arabia, and the eldest of the Sudairi Seven. He served as a minister of education and a minister of interior during his father's reign. He was appointed crown prince when his half-brother Khalid became king.

King Abdullah bin Abdulaziz Al Saud

Reign: 2005 - 2015


Abdullah bin Abdulaziz Al Saud (Arabic: عبد الله بن عبد العزيز آل سعود ʿAbd Allāh ibn ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz Āl Suʿūd; 1 August 1924 – 23 January 2015) was King of Saudi Arabia from 1 August 2005 until his death in 2015. He was Commander of the Saudi National Guard from 1963 to 2010 and Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia from 1982 to 2005 before ascending the throne following the death of his half-brother, King Fahd.

King Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud

Reign: 2015 - Present


Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud (Arabic: سلمان بن عبد العزیز آل سعود Salmān ibn ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz Āl Saʿūd; born 31 December 1935) is King of Saudi Arabia, reigning since 23 January 2015, and previously served as Deputy Governor and then Governor of Riyadh from 1963 to 2011.
